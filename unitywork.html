My big dive into game coding

Since before the release of the SEGA MegaDrive I had wished, hoped, prayed and dreamed of becoming a video game maker.
I has been entranced by the sheer poetry that could be achieved with a video game, even back in the 80s, and dreamed of creating games that people
would adore.
Up until a few years ago I had nothing outside of RPG Maker to try to gain the necessary skills. This didn't work to my benefit and I have never been from
a wealthy family, so it seemed that I had hit a dead end. Until I discovered the Unity engine, which can make creating video games very easily.





The first complete game I created. A horde survival game.

As you can see from the above pictures, I had a lot to learn and a long way to go (I still do). However, I recently moved away from the FPS
style game and moved towards virtual reality. My aim being to make the first ever open world MMORPG that is entirely in virtual reality.
This is a monumental task and will add a lot more to what I need to learn, I already require 3D modelling skills. My 3D models don't look too bad,
but they are far from optimised.
